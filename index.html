About

As a team of dynamic and professional psychologists and therapists we provide counselling and coaching sessions to individuals, couples and families in several languages. We hope we can be of service to those who want to feel confident and balanced even in a challenging, new environment. Our goal is to make you ready and fit to tackle your own challenges, but until then, we are here to provide you support. Feel free to contact us and tell us Your Story.

    • Après l’obtention de mon diplôme de psychologue à l’Université Libre de Bruxelles en orientation psychologie clinique et psychopathologie, j’ai acquis une expérience professionnelle variée. J’ai travaillé dans des centres de consultations pour enfants et en milieu hospitalier (psychiatrie infantino-juvénile). A l’université j’étais déjà fascinée par l’approche systémique selon laquelle nous pouvons mieux comprendre l’individu, si on le considère à travers son environnement, son histoire et ses modèles familiaux. Dans la continuité de mon cursus j’ai suivi une formation de thérapie systémique. Actuellement, afin d’améliorer ma pratique, je poursuis une formation de psychothérapie intégrative de l’enfant. Cette approche affirme la valeur inhérente de chaque individu et utilise une méthode unifiante qui prend en considération les niveaux de fonctionnement affectif, comportemental, cognitif et physiologique. Dans mon travail, il me tient à coeur d’offrir à mes clients un cadre bienveillant et de confiance. Mon objectif est d’assurer une aide psychologique de qualité en français à ceux qui en sentent le besoin. Mes consultations s’adressent à toute personne confrontée à une période difficile : difficultés familiales, conjugales, difficultés d’adaptation; manque d’estime de soi, période de crise…

    • I obtained my master’s degree at the Université Libre de Bruxelles in psychology, specialized in clinical psychology. Since then I have gained experience in various fields, such as child and adolescent psychiatry and worked in consultation centers for children and their parents. I was fascinated by family therapy during university years, an approach that considers the individual as part of its social environment and takes patterns and family history into account. After obtaining my degree I was trained in family therapy and currently I pursue studies in integrative child psychotherapy. The latter one unifies multiple therapeutic approaches and focuses on the values and resources of the individual while taking emotional, behavioural, cognitive, and physiological aspects into account. As confidence is of high importance in therapy, I do my best to provide a trustful and constructive environment. My consultations are open to anyone experiencing a moment of hardship: family and relationship issues, difficulties with adaptation, lack of self confidence… My counsellings are addressed to parents and couples, and I also provide individual consultations for adults, adolescents and children.

    • „No matter how long the storm lasts, the sun always shines behind the clouds.” – Khalil Gibran


    As a counsellor my task is to help clients identify their strengths, move beyond challenging situations, and make them ready to face difficult or threatening events. Sometimes an encouraging word does the trick, as listening with empathy can be an important step towards resolution. At other times objective advice, removed from emotion, helps. There are few hard and fast rules in counselling.

    But given attention, empathy, acceptance and respect, healing can start. Clients are supported in finding ways to connect with their inner strengths – present in each of us – no matter how dark the clouds are which blot out the inner light. I am a cognitive research psychologist graduate of ELTE University of Budapest. I constantly seek new ways to challenge people’s beliefs in order to help them live a more satisfying life.
    I look forward to hearing from you.

    Services

    You can find your own answers in the course of our counselling sessions to many types of questions.
    Our clients typically seek our help with questions like:

    • How can I manage stress better at work?
    • How can I fit into my new work environment?
    • All my friends are far away, how can I deal with this and meet new people?
    • I was shocked by the cultural difference after arriving here. Is there anything I must be aware to mitigate this?
    • My current partner is from Hungary, how will we be able to manage our relationship on a long term? What kind of challenges will we have to face?
    • I feel insecure in my new position and I am far from those who could help. What can I do?
    • Moving here have put some extra stress on our relationship (and family). What could we do to counterbalance its effects?
    • Our kids find it difficult to blend into their new schools. Is there any way we can support them somehow?

    Good to know

    How to get an appointment?
    To reserve a session, please contact the counsellor of your choice via e-mail or mobile phone.

    How can I cancel an appointment?
    Please note that our cancellation policy requires a notification 24/48 hours prior to the already booked appointment.

    How long is a session?
    A private session typically lasts 50 minutes, please try to arrive 5 minutes ahead.

    How much will it cost?
    Please consider that our counselling fees may vary upon counsellor and range between 10.000 to 15.000 HUF for a private session of 50 minutes, and between 20.000 to 30.000 HUF for a couple therapy session of 90 minutes.

    Where would I need to go?
    We are located in the 2nd district: 1024 Budapest, Szász Károly street 6.

    For Organizations

    We, at Expat Coaching Budapest believe that people who come to Hungary from all around the world deserve professional help with their adjustment process on their own language.

    Every one of us strives to keep up a good balance in several aspects of our everyday lives. Since obscurities in the social environment influence our mood & behaviour more than conscious thought patterns, the balance of emotional well-being can be at risk when new to a country.

    We are professional consultants who provide mental-health related services whether life-coaching, counselling or psychotherapy.

    As a ‘side dish’ we have prepared this material to help the adjustment process of expats, immigrants, digital nomads etc. to this unique cultural and social setting, that you call Hungary, and we call Magyarország.

    Crash-course on Hungarian mentality

    What is it?

    Interesting and useful content that can be delivered in various formats and depths: from a 50 mins lecture thru a 3 hrs long workshop to a 2-day-long training programme – it’s our customer’s choice. Yet, no matter what the format is, it’s always delivered in a funny and relaxed way with practical hints and tips for immediate use.

    Why is it good?

    • Boosts confidence
    • Reduces anxiety
    • Broadens your understanding of the locals’ behaviour, emotions and motives
    • Strengthens your ability to control social situations formerly foreign or alien to you
    • Provides opportunity for like-minded people to meet

    Who can apply and how?

    Whether you are …

    • with an organization that aims at helping such communities
    • a representative of a company that wishes to help expatriated employees
    • or an individual seeking professional help
    ask for an appointment for a free-of-charge consultation to discuss your request.
